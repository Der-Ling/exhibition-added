The High-Q Mode of PT Symmetric System Achieves Stable Near-field High-precision Positioning and Navigation

TIE 2025


Abstract

â€”For the challenge of high-precision position ing of unmannedsystemsatcloserange,specificallywithin distances a few times their body length, this paper pro poses a magnetic near-field coupling positioning system combined with PT symmetry. This system leverages the high-Q mode in the weak coupling area to achieve precise positioning. First, the theoretical model established shows that when the coupling strength, gain rate and radiative rate satisfy a specific condition, the corresponding trans mission distance has a high quality factor, and the received energy has amaximumvalue.Asthetransmission distance changes, the received energy intensity quickly attenuates. This nonlinear change in energy intensity with distance is highly advantageous for signal sampling and recogni tion. Based on the high-Q mode condition, a continuous corresponding relationship between the gain regulation quantity and the transmission distance is established, and a dynamic control method for switching the high-Q mode is proposed. This realizes a distance-adjustable positioning and navigation function. Subsequently, the voltage differ ence between the target position and adjacent positions is measured to design and verify the aforementioned model. Ultimately, using an unmanned vehicle as the control ob ject, millimeter-level positioning and navigation accuracy is achieved within a range of 2.5 body lengths.
